{{<exercise 1>}}
Let $\alpha_n:\mathbf{Z}/p\mathbf{Z}\to\mathbf{Z}/p^n\mathbf{Z}$ be the injection of abelian groups given by $\alpha_n(1)=p^{n-1}$, and let $\alpha:A\to B$ be the direct sum of all the $\alpha_n$ (where $A$ is a countable direct sum of copies of $\mathbf{Z}/p\mathbf{Z}$, and $B$ is the direct sum of the $\mathbf{Z}/p^n\mathbf{Z}$). Show that the $p$-adic completion of $A$ is just $A$ but that the completion of $A$ for the topology induced from the $p$-adic topology on $B$ is the direct *product* of the $\mathbf{Z}/p\mathbf{Z}$.
QUESTIONANDANSWERSTRING\
Notice $pA=0$, hence $\hat{A}=A$. On the other hand the induced topology from $B$ has basis $\alpha^{-1}(p^mB)=\bigoplus\limits_{n>m}\mathbf{Z}/p\mathbf{Z}$, with quotient $A/\alpha^{-1}(p^mB)\simeq\bigoplus\limits_{0< n\le m}\mathbf{Z}/p\mathbf{Z}$, hence the completion $\hat{A}=\varprojlim\bigoplus\limits_{0\lt n\le m}\mathbf{Z}/p\mathbf{Z}=\prod_{n\gt 0}\mathbf{Z}/p\mathbf{Z}$.
TOMANDJERRYSTRINGLITERAL\
Deduce that $p$-adic completion is *not* a right-exact functor on the category of all $\mathbf{Z}$-modules.
QUESTIONANDANSWERSTRING\
For the exact sequence $0\to A\to B\to B/\alpha(A)\to 0$, trivial verifications show that the $p$-adic topology on $B/\alpha(A)$ is that induced by the $p$-adic topology on $B$, hence by Corollary 10.3 the completed \[0\to\prod_{n\gt 0}\mathbf{Z}/p\mathbf{Z}\to\hat{B}\to\widehat{B/\alpha(A)}\to0\] is exact, but the $p$-adic completion of $A$ is not $\prod_{n\gt 0}\mathbf{Z}/p\mathbf{Z}$.
{{</exercise>}}

{{<exercise 2>}}
In Exercise 1, let $A_n=\alpha^{-1}(p^nB)$, and consider the exact sequence \[0\to A_n\to A\to A/A_n\to 0\] Show that $\varprojlim$ is not right exact, and compute $\varprojlim^1 A_n$.
QUESTIONANDANSWERSTRING\
The $\varprojlim^1$ of a surjective system is $0$, there is an exact sequence \[\varprojlim A_n\to\varprojlim A\xrightarrow{\pi}\varprojlim A/A_n\to{\varprojlim}^1 A_n\to{\varprojlim}^1 A=0\] it is easy to see $\varprojlim A_n=0$, and $\pi$ is the natural embedding $A=\bigoplus\limits_{n\gt 0}\mathbf{Z}/p\mathbf{Z}\to\prod\limits_{n\gt 0}\mathbf{Z}/p\mathbf{Z}$, hence $\varprojlim^1 A_n\simeq\prod_{n\gt 0}\mathbf{Z}/p\mathbf{Z}/\bigoplus\limits_{n\gt 0}\mathbf{Z}/p\mathbf{Z}$.
{{</exercise>}}

{{<exercise 3>}}
Let $A$ be a Noetherian ring, $\mathfrak{a}$ an ideal and $M$ a finitely-generated $A$-module. Using Krull's Theorem and Exercise 14 of Chapter 3, prove that \[\bigcap_{n=1}^{\infty}\mathfrak{a}^nM=\bigcap_{\mathfrak{m}\supseteq\mathfrak{a}}\ker(M\to M_\mathfrak{m})\] where $\mathfrak{m}$ runs over all maximal ideals containing $\mathfrak{a}$.
QUESTIONANDANSWERSTRING\
By Theorem 10.17 $\bigcap\limits_{n=1}^\infty\mathfrak{a}^nM=\{x:x=\mathfrak{a}x\}=\{x:(x)=\mathfrak{a}(x)\}$, which by Chapter 3, Exercise 14 is $\{x:(x)_\mathfrak{m}=0\text{ for all maximal }\mathfrak{m}\supseteq\mathfrak{a}\}$, hence the result.
TOMANDJERRYSTRINGLITERAL\
Deduce that \[\hat{M}=0\Leftrightarrow\operatorname{Supp}(M)\cap V(\mathfrak{a})=\varnothing\quad(\text{in }\operatorname{Spec}(A)).\] [The reader should think of $\hat{M}$ as the "Taylor expansion" of $M$ transversal to the subscheme $V(\mathfrak{a})$; the above result then shows that $M$ is determined in a neighborhood of $V(\mathfrak{a})$ by its Taylor expansion.]
QUESTIONANDANSWERSTRING\
By Theorem 10.17 $\ker(M\to\hat{M})=\bigcap\limits_{n=1}^\infty\mathfrak{a}^nM$.
{{</exercise>}}

{{<exercise 4>}}
Let $A$ be a Noetherian ring, $\mathfrak{a}$ an ideal in $A$, and $\hat{A}$ the $\mathfrak{a}$-adic completion. For any $x\in A$, let $\hat{x}$ be the image of $x$ in $\hat{A}$. Show that \[x\text{ not a zero-divisor in }A\Rightarrow\hat{x}\text{ not a zero-divisor in }\hat{A}.\]
QUESTIONANDANSWERSTRING\
See the hint below.
TOMANDJERRYSTRINGLITERAL\
Does this imply that \[A\text{ is an integral domain}\Rightarrow\hat{A}\text{ is an integral domain?}\] [Apply the exactness of completion to the sequence $0\to A\xrightarrow{x}A$.]
QUESTIONANDANSWERSTRING\
No, because there can be some zero-divisors which are not in $\hat{A}$, for example with coprime ideals $\mathfrak{a}$ and $\mathfrak{b}$ we have $\hat{A}^{\mathfrak{ab}}\simeq \hat{A}^\mathfrak{a}\times\hat{A}^\mathfrak{b}$.
{{</exercise>}}

{{<exercise 5>}}
Let $A$ be a Noetherian ring and let $\mathfrak{a},\mathfrak{b}$ be ideals in $A$. If $M$ is any $A$-module, let $M^\mathfrak{a}$, $M^\mathfrak{b}$ denote its $\mathfrak{a}$-adic and $\mathfrak{b}$-adic completions respectively. If $M$ is finitely generated, prove that $(M^\mathfrak{a})^\mathfrak{b}\simeq M^{\mathfrak{a}+\mathfrak{b}}$.\
[Take the $\mathfrak{a}$-adic completion of the exact sequence {{<explain>}}whose middle term seems to be missing from the book{{</explain>}} \[0\to\mathfrak{b}^mM\to M\to M/\mathfrak{b}^mM\to 0\] and apply (10.13). Then use the isomorphism \[\varprojlim_m(\varprojlim_n M/(\mathfrak{a}^nM+\mathfrak{b}^mM))\simeq\varprojlim_n M/(\mathfrak{a}^nM+\mathfrak{b}^nM)\] and the inclusions $(\mathfrak{a}+\mathfrak{b})^{2n}\subseteq\mathfrak{a}^n+\mathfrak{b}^n\subseteq(\mathfrak{a}+\mathfrak{b})^n$.]
QUESTIONANDANSWERSTRING\
The $\mathfrak{a}$-adic completion of the sequence in the hint is \[0\to(\mathfrak{b}^m M)^\mathfrak{a}\xrightarrow{\iota^\mathfrak{a}}M^\mathfrak{a}\to(M/\mathfrak{b}^mM)^\mathfrak{a}\to0\] and it is obvious that $\operatorname{im}(\iota^\mathfrak{a})=\mathfrak{b}^mM^\mathfrak{a}$, hence \[(M^\mathfrak{a})^\mathfrak{b}\simeq\varprojlim_m(M/\mathfrak{b}^mM)^\mathfrak{a}\simeq \varprojlim_m(\varprojlim_n M/(\mathfrak{a}^nM+\mathfrak{b}^mM))\] taking the cofinal subsequence we have $(M^\mathfrak{a})^\mathfrak{b}\simeq\varprojlim_n M/(\mathfrak{a}^nM+\mathfrak{b}^nM)$, and taking into account $(\mathfrak{a}+\mathfrak{b})^{2n}\subseteq\mathfrak{a}^n+\mathfrak{b}^n\subseteq(\mathfrak{a}+\mathfrak{b})^n$ we see it is then isomorphic to $\varprojlim M/(\mathfrak{a}+\mathfrak{b})^n=M^{\mathfrak{a}+\mathfrak{b}}$.
{{</exercise>}}

{{<exercise 6>}}
Let $A$ be a Noetherian ring and $\mathfrak{a}$ an ideal in $A$. Prove that $\mathfrak{a}$ is contained in the Jacobson radical of $A$ if and only if every maximal ideal of $A$ is closed for the $\mathfrak{a}$-topology. (A Noetherian topological ring in which the topology is defined by an ideal contained in the Jacobson radical is called a *Zariski ring*. Examples are local rings and (by (10.15)(iv)) $\mathfrak{a}$-adic completions.)
QUESTIONANDANSWERSTRING\
We shall only prove that a maximal ideal $\mathfrak{m}$ closed under $\mathfrak{a}$-topology if and only if  $\mathfrak{a}\subseteq\mathfrak{m}$. The 'if' part is because $A\backslash\mathfrak{m}=\bigcup\limits_{x\in A\backslash\mathfrak{m}}(x+\mathfrak{a})$. For the 'only if' part, let $\mathfrak{m}$ be closed, then $1\not\in\mathfrak{m}$ says $\exists n,1+\mathfrak{a}^n\subseteq A\backslash\mathfrak{m}$, hence $\mathfrak{a}^n\subseteq\mathfrak{m}$, by taking radical we have $\mathfrak{a}\subseteq\mathfrak{m}$.
{{</exercise>}}

{{<exercise 7>}}
Let $A$ be a Noetherian ring, $\mathfrak{a}$ an ideal of $A$, and $\hat{A}$ the $\mathfrak{a}$-adic completion. Prove that $\hat{A}$ is faithfully flat over $A$ (Chapter 3, Exercise 16) if and only if $A$ is a Zariski ring (for the $\mathfrak{a}$-topology).\
[Since $\hat{A}$ is flat over $A$, it is enough to show that \[M\to\hat{M}\text{ injective for all finitely generated }M\Leftrightarrow A\text{ is Zariski;}\] now use (10.19) and Exercise 6.]
QUESTIONANDANSWERSTRING\
Following the hint, $\hat{A}$ faithfully flat is equivalent to $M\to\hat{A}\otimes_A M$ injective for all $M$, which is equivalent to that for all cyclic modules, by Proposition 10.13 is further equivalent to $M\to\hat{M}$ injective.\
If $\mathfrak{a}\subseteq\mathfrak{R}_A$ {{<explain>}}the Jacobson radical{{</explain>}}, then Corollary 10.19 and Theorem 10.17 says $M\to\hat{M}$ injective. If $\mathfrak{a}\not\subseteq\mathfrak{R}_A$, then take maximal ideal $\mathfrak{m}\not\supseteq\mathfrak{m}$ and $M=A/\mathfrak{m}$, then $\operatorname{Supp}(M)=\{\mathfrak{m}\}$ does not intersect $V(\mathfrak{a})$, hence $\hat{M}=0$ by Exercise 3, and $M\to\hat{M}$ is not injective.
{{</exercise>}}

{{<exercise 8>}}
Let $A$ be the local ring of the origin in $\mathbf{C}^n$ (i.e., the ring of all rational functions $f/g\in\mathbf{C}(z_1,\ldots,z_n)$ with $g(0)\neq 0$), let $B$ be the ring of power series in $z_1,\ldots,z_n$ which converge in some neighborhood of the origin, and let $C$ be the ring of formal power series in $z_1,\ldots,z_n$, so that $A\subset B\subset C$. Show that $B$ is a local ring and that its completion for the maximal ideal topology is $C$.
QUESTIONANDANSWERSTRING\
The maximal ideal of $B$ is $\mathfrak{m}=(z_1,\ldots,z_n)$; the other elements are all invertible, so this is the only maximal ideal. Let $Z=\mathbf{C}[z_1,\ldots,z_n]$ and its maximal ideal $\mathfrak{n}=\mathfrak{m}\cap Z$, as $B/\mathfrak{m}^k\simeq Z/\mathfrak{n}^k$, we have $\hat{B}\simeq\hat{Z}=C$.
TOMANDJERRYSTRINGLITERAL\
Assuming that $B$ is Noetherian, prove that $B$ is $A$-flat. [Use Chapter 3, Exercise 17, and Exercise 7 above.]
QUESTIONANDANSWERSTRING\
Notice $A=Z_\mathfrak{n}$ is also Noetherian, hence $C\simeq \hat{A}$ is flat on $A$. As $B$ is Zariski, $C$ is faithfully flat on $B$, hence $B$ is flat on $A$ {{<explain>}}Chapter 3, Exercise 17{{</explain>}}.
{{</exercise>}}

{{<exercise 9>}}
Let $A$ be a local ring, $\mathfrak{m}$ its maximal ideal. Assume that $A$ is $\mathfrak{m}$-adically complete. For any polynomial $f(x)\in A[x]$, let $\overline{f}(x)\in (A/\mathfrak{m})[x]$ denote its reduction $\bmod{\mathfrak{m}}$. Prove *Hensel's lemma*: if $f(x)$ is monic of degree $n$ and if there exist coprime monic polynomials $\overline{g}(x),\overline{h}(x)\in(A/\mathfrak{m})[x]$ of degrees $r,n-r$ with $\overline{f}(x)=\overline{g}(x)\overline{h}(x)$, then we can lift $\overline{g}(x),\overline{h}(x)$ back to monic polynomials $g(x),h(x)\in A[x]$ such that $f(x)=g(x)h(x)$.\
[Assume inductively that we have constructed $g_k(x),h_k(x)\in A[x]$ such that $g_k(x)h_k(x)-f(x)\in\mathfrak{m}^kA[x]$. Then use the fact that since $\overline{g}(x)$ and $\overline{h}(x)$ are coprime we can find $\overline{a}_p(x),\overline{b}_p(x)$, of degrees $\le n-r,r$ respectively, such that $x^p=\overline{a}_p(x)\overline{g}_k(x)+\overline{b}_p(x)\overline{h}_k(x)$, where $p$ is any integer such that $1\le p\le n$. Finally, use the completeness of $A$ to show that the sequences $g_k(x),h_k(x)$ converge to the required $g(x),h(x)$.]
QUESTIONANDANSWERSTRING\
The hint is basically the answer; the inductive step is by writing $g_kh_k-f=\sum m_ix^i$ with $m_i\in\mathfrak{m}$, then take $g_{k+1}=g_k-\sum m_ib_i$ and $h_{k+1}=h_k-\sum m_ia_i$.
{{</exercise>}}

{{<exercise 11>}}
Show that the converse of (10.26) is false, even if we assume that $A$ is local and that $\hat{A}$ is a finitely-generated $A$-module.\
[Take $A$ to be the ring of germs of $C^\infty$ functions of $x$ at $x=0$, and use Borel's theorem that every power series occurs as the Taylor expansion of some $C^\infty$ function.]
QUESTIONANDANSWERSTRING\
The hint is basically the answer; Borel's theorem says $A\to\hat{A}$ is surjective. The maximal ideal of $A$ is $\mathfrak{m}=\{[f]:f(0)=0\}$. By considering Taylor expansion $\mathfrak{m}^n=([x]^n)$, and $/\mathfrak{m}^n\simeq\mathbf{R}[x]/(x^n)$, hence $\hat{A}\simeq\mathbf{R}[[x]]$ is Noetherian. However $[e^{-\frac1{x^2}}]\in\bigcap\limits_{n=1}^\infty\mathfrak{m}^n$, hence $A$ is not Noetherian.
{{</exercise>}}
